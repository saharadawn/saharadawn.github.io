(1) Open Pas

Campaign design for a dance festival, exploring how type can follow the lines of a dancer, becoming visually integrated with the dancer’s form. Open Pas is a festival that aims to make dance more accesible to the public, offering a week of free preformances.

(2) La Maison Volpé

A creatively typeset book using the written work of Natalia Ginzburg. Paired with images from @normanscafelondon. Exploring the concept of taste, both in the literal sense–Ginzburg’s critique of British cuisine–but also, ‘taste’ in the sense of, our own cultural biases that we use to judge other cultures.

(3) 15 Found Photos and Their Staged Counterparts

A 15 object, 30 photo, digital archive documenting the various random objects that exist in my camera roll, and their studio photographed counterparts. Each object was shot on an iPhone in its “found environment” then reshot in a studio setting Exploring how the percieved value of objects is heavily influenced by the way they are presented before us. Coded, designed, and photographed by me.

(4) Crew Zine

The Creative Kids’ first print edition feeaturing an array of talented creatives from around the world. Crew aims to uplift youth creatives, build community, and explore new paths the creative world is going down.

(5) FINAL_072.pdf

FINAL_072.PDF escapes creative block, embodying my rediscovery of the creative process through the lens of the self, the audience, and the product. Imitating the creative process, this book is abstract in nature, acting as a collection of my stream of conciousness. Influenced by Freidrich Hegel’s “Thesis, Antithesis, Synthesis”

(6) street(view)photography

“street(view)photography” is a digital photo exhibition featuring various scenes and compositions captured and pulled from the Google Maps Street View API. Being someone who spends a lot of time just walking through Street View, I wanted to find a way to ‘document’ my findings through a curated and interactive website. It became an interesting intersection between irl photography and the web. Users can view photographs as stills but can also go further to walk around in these scenes and capture their own photographs.
